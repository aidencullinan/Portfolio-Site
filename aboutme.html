About Me

Hi, I’m Aiden Cullinan, a cybersecurity-focused applied computing student with a strong foundation in software development and networking. My journey into tech began with a deep curiosity about how systems work and how they can be both built and broken. Over time, that curiosity grew into a passion for protecting digital infrastructure and creating tools that are both functional and secure.

Currently, I’m pursuing a degree in Applied Computing with a focus on Cybersecurity and a minor in Mathematics at Arizona State University. Alongside my studies, I’ve worked on a range of technical projects, from data-driven apps to vulnerability research, and I continue to sharpen my skills in languages like Java, Python, and C. I created this site not just as a portfolio, but as a space to document my growth, showcase hands-on projects, and reflect on the evolving challenges in tech.

Looking ahead, I plan to build a career at the intersection of software and security, helping organizations stay ahead of threats through proactive design, secure coding, and meaningful innovation. Whether it's through contributing to open-source projects, collaborating on research, or exploring new technologies like AI and blockchain, I'm excited to keep learning and making an impact in the cybersecurity field.

This portfolio site is organized to reflect both my technical capabilities and ongoing learning. The Home page gives a brief introduction and highlights key areas of my work. The About Me page—where you are now—offers a deeper look into my background, goals, and values as a developer and cybersecurity student. The Projects page showcases selected projects that I am working on or have completed, often with video and image demonstrations, explanations, and examples. The Blog section contains write-ups on topics I’ve explored in-depth intended to both document and share my insights with others. On the Resume page, you’ll find an up-to-date summary of my technical skills, work experience, and education. Finally, the Contact page includes a form where visitors can reach out for collaboration, feedback, or networking opportunities. Altogether, this site is both a living record of my academic and professional journey and a gateway for others to connect with me.
